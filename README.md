# R and Data Analysis for Social Scientists

A practical introduction to R and data analysis for social scientists (2026).

## Goals

To provide a broad overview of how to use R for political science research and to highlight the opportunities it offers to researchers, adopting a contemporary approach that integrates AI tools and situates R within a wider research ecosystem.

## Prerequisites

The course is designed for students with no prior experience in R. No background in quantitative methods, statistical software, or programming skills is required. While not mandatory, students are encouraged to bring their laptop with a working installation of RStudio.

## Content

The course is divided into three parts (1 hour each).

- **Part 1 — R Fundamentals**: basic arithmetic and logical operators, key data structures (variables, vectors, dataframes), and how to create, modify, and filter them.
- **Part 2 — Working with Real Data**: installing and loading packages, importing datasets, computing descriptive statistics, creating frequency tables, and performing linear regression analyses. The 2022 Italian election results are used as a running example.
- **Part 3 — Data Visualisation with ggplot2**: the package's layered logic and the main chart types: scatterplots, histograms, barplots, faceted histograms, and countplots.

## Acquired Skills

At the end of the course, students will acquire an understanding of how to use R, its underlying logics, and how they can apply it in their own research.
